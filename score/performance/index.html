How to read this track record

The product is young. The point is transparency first, not polished win-rate marketing.

Early sample

Most detections may still be tracked rather than closed. Treat current performance as a live journal, not a mature track record.

Market beta matters

A bull regime can make long-only detection look better than it really is. Benchmark comparison will be added once enough closed data exists.

Exceptional is rare by design

The highest tier is intentionally strict. Zero recent Exceptional detections means the scanner did not see maximum confluence, not that the tier is unused.

Closure rule

Each detection is tracked according to its category. Watchlist detections are tracked for 7 days, Signal detections for 21 days, and Exceptional detections for 45 days.

About this data

The Score Tool detects confluence on three pillars (Macro, Tech, Psycho) and emits three categories: Exceptional, Signal, and Watchlist. Once emitted, each detection is tracked at +1h, +4h, +24h, +7d and +30d. Direction-correct means the price moved in the predicted direction at the time of the last update.

Prices are checked once per hour. Milestone columns (+24h, +7d) capture the first observation after that horizon, not the exact price at the precise minute. Observed MFE and MAE are the best and worst directional moves seen across these hourly checks, not tick-by-tick maxima.

The simulation above is not a realized PnL curve. It marks each detection at 1 USDC using the latest directional move available. The scanner does not yet build entries, stops or take-profits. What you see here is what happened to the price after each detection. Nothing more, nothing less.
